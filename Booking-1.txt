Since your accounting system is already the financial backbone, the booking system should do much more than just reserve rooms. It should become a complete hospitality, tourism, wildlife, and conservation management platform that automatically generates accounting transactions, forecasts revenue, and measures economic impact.

For lodges, game reserves, national parks, campsites, and tourism operators, I would structure it into the following modules:

# 17. Citizen Work → Data → Trust → Capital Integration

This aligns with your broader economic platform.

For every lodge, campsite, reserve, or tour operator track:

* Visitor volumes
* Revenue
* Tax compliance
* Conservation compliance
* Employment created
* Local sourcing
* Community impact

Then generate:

* Business health score
* Tourism credit score
* Sustainability score
* Financing readiness score

Banks, investors, and government agencies can use these scores to provide financing, incentives, and support.

# 18. AI Agent Features

Allow managers to ask:

* "Which package generated the most revenue this year?"
* "Forecast visitors for the next holiday season."
* "Which rooms have the lowest occupancy?"
* "Which tour operators bring the highest value guests?"
* "How much foreign currency did we earn this month?"
* "What is the profitability of each safari package?"
* "Which game drives are underutilized?"

The AI agent can automatically analyze bookings, accounting data, inventory, and operations to provide recommendations.

This would transform your solution from a booking system into a complete Tourism, Hospitality, Wildlife Conservation, and Economic Intelligence Platform that works seamlessly with the accounting system and provides valuable data to businesses, conservation agencies, and government tourism authorities.
